          <h2>Quick definition</h2>

          <p>

            A bilevel optimization problem has a <strong>leader</strong> (upper level) decision whose feasible set

            is constrained by the optimal response of a <strong>follower</strong> (lower level).

          </p>

          <div class="equation">

            <div class="mono">

              minimize<sub>x</sub> F(x, y) <br/>

              subject to y ∈ argmin<sub>y</sub> { f(x, y) : g(x, y) ≤ 0 },  X constraints

            </div>

          </div>

          <p class="muted">

            This nested structure makes BLO difficult: nonconvexity, nonsmoothness, implicit constraints,

            and multiple local/global follower optima.

          </p>

        </div>

      </div>

          <h3>Why is BLO hard?</h3>

          <ul class="bullets">

            <li><strong>Nested optimization</strong> creates an implicit feasible region.</li>

            <li><strong>Nonconvexity & discontinuity</strong> appear even with simple pieces.</li>

            <li><strong>Multiplicity</strong>: follower may have multiple optima (pessimistic/optimistic).</li>

            <li><strong>Computational complexity</strong> is often high (frequently NP-hard / Σp2-hard in general settings).</li>

          </ul>

        </div>


        <div class="card">

          <h3>Common categories</h3>

          <ul class="bullets">

            <li>Continuous / integer / mixed-integer BLO</li>

            <li>Linear / convex / nonconvex followers</li>

            <li>Single-level reduction (KKT, value function, strong duality)</li>

            <li>Multi-objective BLO (Pareto leader + nested follower)</li>

          </ul>
