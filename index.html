Quick definition

Bilevel programs were first studied by Bracken and McGill [1], motivated by applications in military planning as well as production and marketing decision making. The terms bilevel optimization programming and multilevel optimization programming were introduced later by Candler and Norton [2].

Bilevel optimization (BLO) problems have a hierarchical structure in which an upper-level optimization problem is constrained by the optimal response of a lower-level optimization problem (Eq. \(\eqref{eq:blo}\)) [3].

\begin{equation}\label{eq:blo} \begin{aligned} \min_{x_u}\ & F(x_u, x_l)\\ \text{s.t.}\ & G(x_u, x_l)\le 0,\\ & x_l \in \arg\min_{x_l}\{ f(x_u, x_l): g(x_u, x_l)\le 0 \}. \end{aligned} \end{equation}

where

  • \(x_u\): upper-level (leader) decision vector;
  • \(x_l\): lower-level (follower) decision vector;
  • \(F, G\): leader objective and constraints;
  • \(f, g\): follower objective and constraints;
  • \(\arg\min\) makes the LL optimum set a constraint on \((x_u, x_l)\);
  • Note: equality constraints are often omitted for readability, but can be included at either level.

This nested structure makes BLO difficult: nonconvexity, nonsmoothness, implicit constraints, and multiple local/global follower optima.
